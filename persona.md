# User Persona: Bengaluru Manufacturing SME Owner

## Persona Snapshot
**Name:** Suresh Kumar
**Role:** Owner-operator, CNC machining unit
**Age:** 42-55
**Location:** Peenya Industrial Area, Bengaluru
**Business:** Runs a 15-30 person CNC machining shop supplying automotive and industrial parts; second-generation family business, ~₹3-8 crore annual turnover

## Background
Suresh took over the business from his father 12 years ago and has grown it steadily through relationships with 2-3 anchor customers and a rotating base of smaller trading clients. He has no finance degree, no dedicated accountant on payroll (uses a part-time bookkeeper who visits twice a month for GST filing), and manages pricing, procurement, and collections almost entirely from memory and instinct built over two decades on the shop floor.

## Goals
- Know quickly when raw material (steel, aluminum) cost changes should trigger a price change, and by how much.
- Avoid end-of-month cash flow surprises — know which customers are likely to pay late before it becomes a crisis.
- Make supplier and inventory decisions based on actual upcoming demand, not gut feel or habit.
- Spend less time in Excel trying to piece together what's happening across orders, costs, and payments.
- Get a straight answer with a reason behind it — not a dashboard full of numbers he has to interpret himself.

## Frustrations & Pain Points
- Cash flow problems sneak up on him — he's part of the 82% of businesses where cash flow mismanagement, not lack of sales, is the real threat [web:84].
- He's part of the national pattern where SMEs seeking capital are mostly doing so to survive a cash crunch, not to expand [web:88].
- His pricing decisions lag behind cost changes because he only notices a supplier's rate increase after the invoice arrives, not when the market shifts.
- He has invoices, quotes, and past price lists sitting in PDFs and Excel sheets across email and WhatsApp, with no way to make that data "talk."
- He's cautious about new tools — burned before by software that promised automation but required more manual setup than it saved.
- News about material costs or industry shifts (like EV parts displacing traditional ICE components) reaches him informally, through supplier gossip or trade association WhatsApp groups, often too late to act on.

## Technology Comfort
- Comfortable with WhatsApp, basic Excel, and Tally for GST/invoicing.
- Not comfortable with BI tools, complex dashboards, or anything requiring a learning curve.
- Owns a smartphone and checks business updates on it throughout the day; desktop use is limited to invoicing and email.

## What He Needs From This Product
- Plain-language explanations, not jargon — "why" matters more to him than "what."
- A weekly rhythm he can trust — one ranked list of what to act on, not a live feed he has to babysit.
- The ability to ask a question in his own words and get a grounded answer referencing his actual orders and customers.
- Confidence to test a decision ("what if I raise prices 5%?") before committing, since a wrong call with a key customer can hurt a relationship built over years.
- A way to bring in what he already has — old price lists, supplier quotes, invoices — without re-entering everything from scratch.

## A Day in Suresh's Life (Relevant Snapshot)
Suresh starts his morning walking the shop floor, checking on active orders, before sitting down with tea to go through WhatsApp messages from suppliers and customers. If a steel price hike hits the news, he usually hears about it secondhand and reacts a few days late — often after he's already quoted a job at the old rate. By month-end, he's frequently surprised by how tight cash is, despite a full order book, because two or three customers paid 20-30 days late.

## Secondary Persona (Brief)
**The Part-Time Bookkeeper/Accountant** — visits or consults periodically, doesn't use the platform daily but may receive a weekly digest/report export to reconcile with GST filings. Values simplicity and accuracy over interactivity.

## Design Implications
- Every screen must lead with a plain-language explanation before any number or chart.
- Mobile-first — Suresh checks things on his phone between shop floor tasks.
- Minimize setup friction — Excel/PDF import must feel as easy as forwarding a WhatsApp attachment.
- Trust is earned incrementally — recommendations need visible reasoning (not black-box output) since he's cautious about tools overpromising.
- Weekly cadence over real-time overload — he wants a decision point, not a stream of alerts to monitor constantly.
